1.
O Lord, my heart is free from pride;
conceit my eyes cannot abide.
I do not look for what would be
too great, too wonderful for me.

2.
No! Like a child that, weaned at last,
lies in its mother’s arms at rest,
no longer fretting anxiously,
my soul is quieted in me.

3.
Hope in the Lord, O Israel;
he surely will make all things well.
For his great wisdom, him adore;
trust him both now and evermore.
